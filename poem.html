Too Distracted by Cookies

Poem by Lina Park

The ticking of the clock was a dreary and dreadful sound.
“Tick! Tock! Tick! Tock!” it shrieked at me with its sharp hands ablaze.
It taunted my shackled fate of imprisonment at the hands of the corporate hound.
The meeting room seemed particularly drab today as voices became a jumbled haze.

There was only one reason for my lack of focus and I can’t say I’m proud of it, alright?
But the thoughts of ooey gooey filling encased by a golden brown cocoon ravished my mind.
It was almost as if I could taste the chocolate chips melting against my tastebuds with every bite.
And knowing last night’s batch of cookies were sitting on my kitchen counter was like torture defined.

Time seemed to stand still as my internal battle for cookies raged on with a burning desire.
So all I could do was count the ticks and the tocks of the very clock that I despised.

I think it’s clear to say that I’m just too distracted by cookies, the only thing that will calm my fire.
